# References: What Is a Digital Footprint?

1. [Digital Footprint](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_footprint) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive overview of digital footprints, including active and passive traces left by internet users, how data is collected, and the long-term implications for privacy and identity.

2. [Online Identity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Online_identity) - Wikipedia - Explains how people construct and manage their identities in digital spaces, including avatars, usernames, and profile pictures, and how these shape how others perceive them online.

3. [Geotagging](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geotagging) - Wikipedia - Describes how geographic metadata is embedded in photos and other digital content, how GPS coordinates are stored automatically by devices, and the privacy risks of sharing geotagged images.

4. *Digital Citizenship in Action: Empowering Students to Engage in Online Communities* by Kristen Mattson, International Society for Technology in Education (ISTE), 2017 - Practical guide for teaching students how to build positive digital identities and manage their online presence responsibly.

5. *Growing Up in Public: Coming of Age in a Digital World* by Devorah Heitner, TarcherPerigee, 2024 - Examines how young people navigate digital identity, reputation, and the permanent nature of online content, with guidance for families and educators.

6. [Digital Footprint and Identity](https://www.commonsense.org/education/digital-citizenship/topic/digital-footprint-and-identity) - Common Sense Education - Lesson plans and resources helping students understand how their digital identity is shaped by the content they share and how they interact online.

7. [The Power of Digital Footprints](https://www.commonsense.org/education/digital-citizenship/lesson/the-power-of-digital-footprints) - Common Sense Education - A classroom lesson teaching students that every photo, message, and activity creates a record that others might see, building awareness of online consequences.

8. [NetSmartz Home](https://www.missingkids.org/netsmartz/home) - National Center for Missing & Exploited Children - Free age-appropriate resources including videos, games, and lessons teaching children ages 5 to 17 about online safety, digital footprints, and responsible internet use.

9. [Be Internet Awesome](https://beinternetawesome.withgoogle.com/en_us) - Google - A comprehensive program including the Interland game and ISTE-aligned lesson plans that teaches kids to share with care and manage their digital footprint through interactive activities.

10. [Digital Citizenship](https://mediasmarts.ca/tag/categories/digital-citizenship) - MediaSmarts - Canada's centre for digital and media literacy offering grade-specific lessons on digital footprint awareness, privacy management, and responsible online behavior for young learners.
